WindSol

A device that predicts your solar and wind energy cost savings.

What is WindSol?

During the initial brainstorming session, our team decided to create WindSol - a system for household owners who want an easy way to estimate the energy savings they could make by installing a green energy solution in a particular location.

The WindSol periodically collects data from its sensors, stores them in its memory, allows the user to offload the data from the device and upload the measurements to the cloud for further analysis.

The aim of the WindSol project was to design an inexpensive and portable measurement device to estimate the amount of solar irradiance and wind energy in remote locations.

By placing this device in multiple different locations, for a fixed time period at each, and collecting data using omnidirectional sensors it would be possible to determine the optimal location and orientation at which to place a solar panel or wind turbine installation for maximal efficiency.

Sensory information collection

The station is a small weatherproof device that gathers data about sunlight intensity (from multiple directions) and wind speed at regular intervals. It is placed outdoors such that the collected data represents the potential of a solar panel/wind turbine installation.

Android App

A smartphone app connects to the station over Bluetooth, downloads data, and uploads it to a server in the cloud.

Web App

The data is then aggregated by the backend server. Web interface provides access to charts and statistics as well as recommendations as to whether an installation of solar panels/wind turbines would pay off.

Does it pay off?

Currently, a lot of people might want to install solar/wind power elements on their roofs, but are worried about the exact savings that their household would have. Although some companies already provide estimates based on the location of the customer, the estimates may vary a lot since they are not based on readings from the actual roof that the customer is going to use.

Greener future

In the future, the stations could be leased by green energy suppliers who are interested in selling their equipment to the customer afterwards.

Companies that sell green power solutions to households might use this device to help potential customers estimate their power savings. This might convince people to install these solutions in their households. Since the monitoring station module is relatively cheap, this could work at a large scale, without the need for large investments. The module would be simple enough to be installed by the household owner without any prior experience.
